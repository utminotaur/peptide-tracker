GLP-1 Dosing Guide

Retatrutide · Tirzepatide · Semaglutide
Educational reference only. This is not medical advice. Semaglutide and tirzepatide are prescription medications; retatrutide is investigational and not FDA-approved. Dosing decisions, titration timing, and use of any GLP-1 class drug must be made with a qualified prescriber.

Overview

GLP-1 receptor agonists slow gastric emptying, increase satiety, and improve glycemic control. Three options dominate the conversation, with meaningful differences in receptor activity, half-life, weight-loss potency, and regulatory status:

  • Retatrutide — triple GLP-1 / GIP / glucagon agonist. Investigational, Phase 3. Greatest trial weight loss to date (~24% at 48 weeks) and the most popular choice in the peptide-community space, despite lacking FDA approval and quality controls. The glucagon arm is the suspected driver of higher fat-loss potency vs the dual agonists. ~6-day half-life.
  • Tirzepatide — dual GLP-1 / GIP agonist. FDA-approved (Mounjaro for T2D, Zepbound for weight). ~5-day half-life. Greater weight loss than semaglutide in head-to-head trials (SURMOUNT vs STEP).
  • Semaglutide — pure GLP-1 agonist. FDA-approved (Ozempic for T2D, Wegovy for weight management). ~7-day half-life. Longest real-world track record, broadest insurance coverage.

General Titration Principles

  • Always start at the lowest dose and titrate upward only when the prior dose is well tolerated for at least 4 weeks.
  • Slower titration = fewer GI side effects. There is no benefit to rushing.
  • If a step causes intolerable nausea or other GI symptoms, hold or step back rather than push through.
  • The "lowest effective dose" is often what works best long-term — many people maintain on intermediate doses rather than the maximum.
  • Inject subcutaneously, same day each week, rotating sites (abdomen, thigh, upper arm).

Side Effect Management

  • Nausea — most common, especially during titration. Smaller, lower-fat meals; eat slowly; protein first.
  • Constipation or diarrhea — fiber 25–35 g/day, hydration, magnesium glycinate.
  • Reflux / belching — avoid lying down within 2 hr of eating.
  • Fatigue — usually reflects underfeeding; ensure adequate protein and electrolytes.
  • "Sulfur burps" — common with tirzepatide. Reducing fat/dairy or adding peppermint oil capsules helps for many.
  • Anti-nausea (ondansetron) PRN per prescriber for breakthrough nausea.

Critical Adjuncts (build muscle preservation around the drug)

  • Protein: 1.6–2.2 g/kg bodyweight daily — non-negotiable for keeping muscle while losing fat.
  • Resistance training: 2–3 sessions/week, hitting major movements. The single most important variable for body composition on GLP-1.
  • Creatine monohydrate: 5 g daily.
  • Electrolytes: sodium, potassium, magnesium daily — appetite suppression often leads to under-consumption.
  • Fiber: 25–35 g/day to manage GI side effects and satiety.
  • B-complex: activated form daily — eating less means fewer dietary B vitamins.
  • Vitamin D3 + K2: 5,000 IU + 180 mcg/day with fat.

Cautions and Contraindications

  • Pancreatitis risk — discontinue and seek care for severe abdominal pain.
  • Avoid in personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN2.
  • Gallbladder issues can occur, particularly at higher doses or rapid weight loss.
  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding — contraindicated; stop ≥2 months before planned conception.
  • Hypoglycemia risk increases when stacked with insulin or sulfonylureas.
  • Often held 1+ week before surgery (anesthesia + delayed gastric emptying = aspiration risk).
  • Stopping abruptly can cause appetite/weight rebound — discuss tapering with prescriber.

When to Hold or Reduce

  • Severe persistent nausea or vomiting interfering with hydration.
  • Signs of pancreatitis (severe upper abdominal pain radiating to back).
  • Significant dehydration.
  • Hospitalization or major illness.
  • Upcoming surgery (per anesthesiologist guidance).

Switching Between Compounds

Going from one GLP-1 class drug to another is common. General approach: stop the current drug, wait one full half-life cycle (5–7 days for tirzepatide, 7–10 days for semaglutide), then start the new one at its lowest titration step — even if you were on a higher equivalent of the prior drug. Re-titrate as if naïve.

Comparing the Three (rough trial averages)

  • Retatrutide 12 mg: ~24% weight loss at 48 weeks (Phase 2 obesity trial).
  • Tirzepatide 15 mg: ~21% weight loss at 72 weeks (SURMOUNT-1).
  • Semaglutide 2.4 mg: ~15% weight loss at 68 weeks (STEP-1).

Real-world results vary widely with diet, training, sleep, and adherence. The peptide community has gravitated to retatrutide because of the higher potency and the additional fat-loss-favoring glucagon arm — accepting the tradeoff of unregulated sourcing and more pronounced GI effects.

Always work with a qualified clinician — especially for retatrutide, which lacks formal approval and quality controls. Bloodwork (lipid panel, A1c, basic metabolic panel, lipase if symptomatic) every 3–6 months is reasonable on long-term use.
